### **Fundamental Landscape (6–12 Month Outlook)**

#### **1. SGOV (iShares 0-3 Month Treasury Bond ETF)**
*   **Role:** Capital preservation and liquidity.
*   **Fundamental Context:** SGOV provides exposure to ultra-short-term U.S. Treasuries. Its primary driver is the Federal Funds Rate. While it offers a stable yield (currently ~3.85%), this yield is highly sensitive to Federal Reserve policy.
*   **Impact:** If the Fed continues to cut rates over the next 12 months, the yield on SGOV will decrease. However, it remains the safest vehicle for your "house fund" capital, as it is immune to the volatility that affects equities.

#### **2. VTI (Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF)**
*   **Role:** Long-term capital appreciation.
*   **Fundamental Context:** VTI captures the entirety of the U.S. equity market. Recent data shows strong inflows into U.S. equities, even amidst concerns over AI concentration.
*   **Impact:** With a 4–5 year window for a house down payment, VTI carries "sequence of returns" risk. While it has historically provided strong growth (12–17% annualized over recent periods), it is subject to significant drawdowns (e.g., -19.5% in 2022). For the portion of your $78,000 earmarked for the house, high exposure to VTI could be risky if a market correction coincides with your purchase date.

#### **3. VXUS (Vanguard Total International Stock ETF)**
*   **Role:** Global diversification.
*   **Fundamental Context:** VXUS has shown high volatility but has outperformed in specific cycles (e.g., 2025). It provides a hedge against U.S.-specific economic slowdowns.
*   **Impact:** International markets are currently sensitive to global growth trajectories and currency fluctuations. It is a suitable long-term holding for your retirement goal but adds complexity to a mid-term house-saving strategy.
